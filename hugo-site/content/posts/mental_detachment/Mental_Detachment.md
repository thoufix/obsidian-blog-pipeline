# 🧘 Minimal Detachment Checklist  
*A quick guide to avoid reacting, avoid caring too much, and keep peace.*

---

## ✅ Before Meeting / Travel
- [ ] Remind yourself: *"Their mood ≠ my responsibility."*
- [ ] Set a mental boundary: *I will not solve their confusion, plan changes, or drama.*
- [ ] Decide the **minimum energy level** you’ll give them today.
- [ ] Visualise yourself being calm & neutral.

---

## 🧍 During Interaction
### Keep Conversations Neutral
- [ ] Stick to **short, factual replies**  
  → “Yes ok.” / “We’ll see.” / “Up to you.” / “Not sure yet.”
- [ ] Avoid giving opinions they can challenge.
- [ ] Avoid engaging in their “flexing”, “joking”, or “plan changing.”

### Emotional Detachment Moves
- [ ] Don’t take their words personally — *it’s not about you.*
- [ ] Don’t defend, don’t justify — **no emotional investment**.
- [ ] When irritated, silently think: *"Not my problem."*
- [ ] Keep your tone soft, expression blank.

### Avoiding Someone Without Being Obvious
- [ ] Stay polite but **short**.
- [ ] Sit away without making it a big deal.
- [ ] Engage more with Shayan/Doody instead of them.
- [ ] When they talk, nod once and say: *"Ah okay."*  
- [ ] Keep yourself “busy”: phone, baby, bag, looking around.

---

## 🧠 Thought Reset (Use when anger rises)
- [ ] Pause 3 seconds before replying.
- [ ] Ask yourself: *"Will this matter in 24 hours?"*
- [ ] Remind: *"I don’t need to win anything here."*
- [ ] Focus on **your day**, not their behaviour.

---

## 👤 After Interaction
- [ ] Do something grounding: water, music, deep breath.
- [ ] Tell Doody only the necessary parts, avoid reliving drama.
- [ ] Don’t overthink — you already handled it.

---

## 🛡️ Golden Rules of Detachment (Quick Reminders)
- [ ] **I don’t have to explain my plans to everyone.**
- [ ] **Their confusion is not my burden.**
- [ ] **I choose peace over reaction.**
- [ ] **Not everyone deserves my emotional energy.**
- [ ] **Being neutral is not rude — it’s self-preservation.**

---

## 📌 Use This Especially When…
- Relatives keep changing plans  
- Someone tries to control your schedule  
- A cousin flexes or irritates you  
- Someone tries to rush you without a valid reason  
- You feel your day being disturbed by others’ moods  

---

## 📝 Notes
- This checklist is for *detachment*, not anger.  
- You’re not avoiding people permanently — only the emotional load.
